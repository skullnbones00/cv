"The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog" is an English-language pangram. This is a sentence that contains all of the letters of the alphabet. The "quick brown fox" is commonly used for touch-typing practice, testing typewriters and computer keyboards, and displaying examples of fonts. It can be used when all the letters in the alphabet need to be shown. Because it is a short sentence which makes sense it has become widely known. "The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog" is an example of a "perfect panagram." In which a perfect panagram is a sentence were every letter in the alphabet is used in a sentence ONCE.
